CSIT 6000N Course Project-Online Healthcare

Phase 1

Introduction:

The advent of the internet and its rapid penetration into every aspect of our lives has redefined the way we interact, work, and access information. One industry that has been revolutionized by the power of the digital world is healthcare. Online healthcare, a fusion of technology and medicine, has the potential to transform the patient experience, making healthcare more accessible, efficient, and personalized than ever before.


Need-Finding:

In conclusion, here are 3 main questions need to be solved:

  • Afraid of cross infection
  • Waiting time too long
  • Elderly can’t be treated in time

Point of View::

You are someone who concerns:

  • your health and wellbeing
  • the risks of cross infection
  • long wait times at hospital
  • the potential for delayed treatment

User:

As a user, you have access to a range of medical services, from consultations with qualified doctors to diagnostic tests and medication prescriptions.

You can access these services from anywhere, without having to physically present at any medical facility.


Need:

The system allows users to schedule appointments, speak to doctors, and receive medical advice at any time to avoid cross infection and long wait time.

In addition, it will provides users with access to medical records and a personal wallet which ensures a clear understanding of their health status and the costs associated.


Insights:

This online medical system provides users with peace of mind and confidence in their ability to take care of their health. They appreciate the convenience and flexibility of being able to access medical care from anywhere, at any time, and the system's focus on safety and hygiene helps to alleviate any concerns they may have about cross infection.

Evaluation Online Healthcare System Prototype - Survey Overview

Questionnaire Design:

  • made through an online survey platform
  • Consisted of 10 questions, within 2 mins to ensure a higher response rate

Measurement Goals:

  • Attitudes: Evaluated through questions about preferences, feature importance, and likelihood to recommend the system
  • Behaviors: Assessed by questions related to the actual usage of certain features (e.g., payment and wallet, chatbot consultations)
Key findings from survey

Important Features:

  • Remote consulting with doctors (90%)
  • Make appointments (80%)
  • Prescription history and view (70%)

Medication records (60%)

  • Security & Accessibility:
  • 80% of respondents find a secure and easy login/registration process important or very important
  • 70% of respondents find having an elderly mode important or very important

Additional Insights:

  • 60% of respondents are willing to use a payment and wallet feature
  • 80% of respondents would like to search, rate, and comment on doctors
  • 70% of respondents would use a chatbot for quick consultations and basic health queries
Heuristic Analysis

Consistency:

  • Internal consistency throughout menus and sub menus by positioning items relatively in the same place
  • External consistency is maintained by standard web and app design Iconography and Images that most users are already familiar with

Minimalism:

  • The application uses a clean design, omits any information that is not necessary and ensures white space while reading important information (prescriptions, doctor descriptions, ratings, etc), to not overwhelm them with a lot of content.

Freedom:

  • Privacy was a major concern for users when we did our phase 1 evaluation.
  • A delete history button has therefore been added in the accounts option, so that patients can delete any information they don’t want us to store.
  • They can also delete their ratings

Flexibility:

  • Patients who might not be sure about who they should consult have two options:
  • Describe their problem in the chat and ask for suggestions.
  • Go through the department description, in case the users are not comfortable sharing personal/sensitive information.

Recognition and Mapping:

  • Recognition has been for the users by using standard web and app design Iconography and Images that most users are already familiar with.
  • This also provides mapping from real world phenomenons to the application

Visibility:

  • In order to give control back to the users we ensure the users always have access to their entire medical and prescription history.
  • They can selectively give access to their history to any doctor they wish.
  • They can ask for updated on the prescription right form the app.

Error Prevention and Recovery:

  • Error prevention is ensured by user ratings for doctors.
  • The users can only rate those doctors they have consulted with using the platform
  • Ratings also ensure error recovery as the platform and hospitals can use patient reviews to decide whether to host a doctor or not.

Help:

  • We aim to both proactive and reactive help approaches.
  • Proactive help to help users answer Frequently asked health questions.
  • Reactive help for questions related to using the application, and questions not in the FAQ’s
Usability Testing

Goals and Target Audience:

  • Assess ease of use for remote consulting and appointment scheduling
  • Evaluate user satisfaction with prescription history and medication records management
  • Determine the effectiveness of the payment and wallet feature
  • Measure the user-friendliness of elderly mode
  • Gauge user experience with searching, rating, and commenting on doctors
  • Target audience: adults of various age groups, including the elderly, with different levels of technical proficiency and various health conditions

Testing Process:

  • After introducing all the features of the app, let the users use it themselves
  • Number of participants: 20
  • Testing environment: remote testing via video conferencing
  • Duration: 1 hour per participant

Key Findings

  • Difficulty in navigating the appointment scheduling process
  • Confusion regarding medication records management
  • Inaccessibility of elderly mode for older users

Suggestions

  • Simplify the appointment scheduling process with a more intuitive interface
  • Improve the medication records management feature with clearer labels and instructions
  • Enhance the elderly mode with larger text and simplified navigation
